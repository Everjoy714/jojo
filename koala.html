KOALA

The koala, sometimes inaccurately called the koala bear, is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only representative of the family Phascolarctidae. Its closest living relatives are the wombats.

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus), sometimes inaccurately called the koala bear,an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia.

WHERE ARE THEY FOUND?

Koalas are found in eastern and southeastern Australia, specifically in the eucalyptus forests of Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, and a small area of South Australia. They are endemic to this region, meaning they are only found naturally in these areas.
