WARTHOG

The common warthog is a wild member of the pig family found in grassland, savanna, and woodland in sub-Saharan Africa. In the past, it was commonly treated as a subspecies of P. aethiopicus, but today that scientific name is restricted to the desert warthog of northern Kenya, Somalia, eastern Ethiopia.

WHERE ARE THEY FOUND?

Warthogs are primarily found in sub-Saharan Africa, particularly in the savanna and grassland areas. They are native to the continent, with common warthogs inhabiting a wide range of savanna, grassland, and woodland habitats in the region.
